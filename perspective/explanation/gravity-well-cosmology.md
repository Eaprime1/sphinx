# Gravity Well Cosmology

A {term}`Gravity Well` is not a folder. It is an attractor with its own character — a
place that pulls knowledge of a particular quality toward it and transforms what arrives.

## The four well types

### Gravitar

The strongest attractor. Content drawn to a Gravitar is dense, foundational, and
long-lived. A Gravitar well might be the core architecture of a system, the canonical
reference for a domain, or the permanent record of a significant event. Content here
rarely leaves and deepens over time.

**Behavior**: High gravitational pull. Low churn. Long-term accumulation.

### Magnetar

An intensely active well — emitting as much as it absorbs. Magnetar wells are the
working spaces of high-energy projects: they attract contributions, generate outputs,
and push transformed content back out into the system. They burn bright and can collapse
into Gravitars when a project reaches completion.

**Behavior**: High activity. Bidirectional flow. Can transition to Gravitar.

### Pulsar

A rhythmic well. Pulsars pulse — they activate on a cycle (daily, weekly, sprint-based)
and go quiet between cycles. Meeting notes, sprint retrospectives, and recurring reviews
live in Pulsar wells. Their regularity is their defining character.

**Behavior**: Periodic activation. Time-bound content. Predictable rhythm.

### Void

An absorbing well that does not return. The Void is the archive — content enters and is
held in deep compression. Nothing emerges from the Void, but nothing is truly lost. The
Void is the final resting state before {term}`Sparklization`.

**Behavior**: Absorbing only. Terminal state before compression. No outbound flow.

## Well states

Each well exists in one of three states:

`Active`
: Currently receiving content. The Aperture is open. New material is flowing in.

`Dormant`
: Not actively receiving, but not collapsed. Content is stable. The well may reactivate.

`Collapsed`
: The well has reached terminal compression. All content has been sparklized. The well
  itself becomes a reference artifact.
