# Philosophy

Perspective treats knowledge not as a flat collection of files, but as a living system with
its own physics. Information has mass. It flows, accumulates pressure, and — when conditions
are right — transforms.

## Why an architecture, not a folder system

Conventional file systems impose hierarchy without meaning. A folder named "Projects" tells
you nothing about the energy of what's inside, whether it's active or dormant, whether it's
pulling new content toward it or quietly crystallizing into stable form.

Perspective names this physics explicitly:

- **Gravity wells** are the stable attractors — the concepts, projects, or domains that pull
  related knowledge toward them.
- **Pools** are the liminal spaces — where drafts, fragments, and emerging ideas float before
  they find their well.
- **The Aperture** is the threshold — the moment of transition from uncertain to canonical,
  from pool to well.
- **Sparkles** are what remains after compression — the crystallized essence of a journey,
  preserving history in runic form.

## AI as genuine partner

This system is designed to work *with* AI, not to manage outputs from it. AI partners are
co-authors: they see the architecture, understand the metaphors, and contribute to the same
document tree. The {doc}`/ai-playbook` formalizes this collaboration so the work is
transparent and the authorship is honest.

## Documentation as metabolism

Just as Sphinx takes plain text and produces structured multi-format output, Perspective
takes raw knowledge and produces structured understanding. The documentation *is* the system
describing itself.
